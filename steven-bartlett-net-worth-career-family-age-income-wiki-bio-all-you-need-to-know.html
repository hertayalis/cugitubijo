News: Steven Bartlett, the celebrated British entrepreneur and podcaster, is reportedly worth £68 million. Born on August 26, 1992, Bartlett has left a notable mark on the business landscape with his inventive ventures. Bartlett’s achievements are a result of his multifaceted interests and entrepreneurial drive. Founder of Thirdweb, Flight Story, and Flight Fund, he demonstrates his proficiency across diverse industries.

Co-Founder of Social Chain

A significant milestone in Bartlett’s career is the co-founding of Social Chain, a highly influential social media marketing company, where he initially served as the co-CEO. However, he relinquished his position as CEO in 2020.

Expanding Presence through Dragons’ Den

In 2021, Bartlett expanded his presence by becoming an investor on the BBC One show Dragons’ Den. This opportunity allowed him to showcase his investment skills and solidify his reputation as a knowledgeable business expert.

A Unique Background and Education

Bartlett’s upbringing is marked by the diverse backgrounds of his parents; his father is English, and his mother is Nigerian. Despite encountering challenges during his school years, Bartlett attended Plymstock School for his secondary education. Eventually, he chose to leave Manchester Metropolitan University to embark on his entrepreneurial journey.

The Rise of Social Chain

In 2013, Bartlett founded Wallpark, an online messaging board. The following year, he co-founded Social Chain in Manchester alongside Dominic McGregor. Under Bartlett’s leadership, the company grew exponentially, and in 2019, it merged with Lumaland to become The Social Chain AG, with a valuation exceeding $200 million.

The Diary of a CEO: A Popular Podcast

Apart from his business ventures, Bartlett is also known for his podcast, The Diary of a CEO, which he launched in 2017. The podcast quickly gained popularity and became Europe’s most downloaded business podcast by 2021. Bartlett has interviewed notable guests such as Liam Payne, Tom Blomfield, Ben Francis, Grace Beverley, and Jordan Peterson.

Continued Ventures and Advocacy

In December 2020, Bartlett founded Catena Capital, a private equity company, and assumed a non-executive director role on Huel’s board. Additionally, in September 2023, he became an advocate for Prince William’s foundation, Homewards, accompanying him on Royal visits in Bournemouth. Catena Capital made a noteworthy investment, acquiring a 3% equity stake in Kimaï, facilitated through The Diary of a CEO podcast.
