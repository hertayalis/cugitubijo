Early Life and Military Service

George Strait was born on May 18, 1952, in Poteet, Texas. He grew up on his father’s cattle ranch and became interested in music at a young age. Hank Williams, Merle Haggard, and George Jones were among his musical influences.

He joined a rock band in high school and then a country band while serving in the United States Army in Hawaii. He married his high school girlfriend, Norma, in 1971, and the couple has two children: Jenifer and George Jr.

College Education and The Ace in the Hole Band

Strait attended Southwest Texas State University after being discharged from the Army and earned a degree in agriculture there in 1979.

He also established The Ace in the Hole Band, which gained popularity on the Texas honky-tonk circuit. They recorded several singles but were unable to gain major label notice. Strait opted to pursue a solo career, signing with MCA Records in 1981.

Breakthrough Success and Record-Breaking Albums

Strait’s debut album, Strait Country, was released in 1981 and featured his first breakthrough single, “Unwound.” The album was hailed for its traditional country style, establishing Strait as a rising star. Strait from the Heart, his second album, was published in 1982 and featured his first number-one song, “Fool Hearted Memory”.
