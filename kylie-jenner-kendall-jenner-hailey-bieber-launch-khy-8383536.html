Kylie Jenner Twinned in All-Leather Looks With Kendall Jenner and Hailey Bieber

June 2024 Â· 2 minute read

Great minds dress alike, at least according to Kylie Jenner and her group of girlfriends.

On Thursday, the mogul stepped out to celebrate the launch of her fashion brand Khy in Santa Monica while wearing head-to-toe black leather that matched the outfits of her closest supporters,Kendall Jenner and Hailey Bieber. The three girls headed to dinner at Giorgio Baldi (where else?), and for the occasion, Kylie wore a black leather halter crop top with motto jacket-inspired padding and a zipper down the front. She paired her tiny shirt with matching high-waisted straight-leg trousers, black strappy sandals, a tiny bag from The Row, and black rectangular shades.

Beauty-wise, she styled her dark hair in tousled waves and finished off her glam with an ultra-glossy lip, smoky eyes, and glowing skin.

Her older sister Kendall followed suit in an equally sleek leather look that consisted of a strapless midi dress paired with sheer black Calzedonia tights, a black clutch, and coordinating kitten heels. Her dark hair was swept up into a messy updo with face-framing bangs, and she opted for a minimal makeup look.

Last but certainly not least, Hailey slipped into a strapless micro-minidress, which she layered over a black sheer mock neck long-sleeved top and Calzedonia tights. Jimmy Choo pumps, a tennis anklet, and a Bottega Veneta Falcon clutch bag accessorized her outfit while she stuck to her signature hairstyle of a slicked-back bun with a middle part.

Earlier this week, Kylie officially announced the launch of her new fashion brand, which will be available to shop on Nov. 1. According to a press release, the brand be inspired by Jenner's personal style and will "embody both futurism and timelessness, defying conventional fashion norms and redefining the limits of creativity." Khy's debut collection will feature 12 pieces available in sizes XXS to 4X created in partnership with leather brand Namilia and its founders Nan Li and Emilia Pfohl.
